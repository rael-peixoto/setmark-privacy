Setmark — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 4, 2026

Setmark is a workout tracking app. This policy explains, in plain terms, what happens to your data when you use it.

Short version

Setmark does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any of your data. Everything you enter — exercises, routines, workout history, and settings — is stored only on your own device. Setmark has no servers, no user accounts, no analytics, and no third-party tracking of any kind.

What data Setmark stores, and where

  • Exercises, routines, workout logs, and app settings are saved locally on your device using standard on-device storage (AsyncStorage). This data never leaves your device and is not accessible to Setmark's developer or anyone else.
  • Exercise photos: if you choose to add a photo to an exercise, Setmark opens your device's photo library so you can pick an image. That photo is referenced locally on your device only — it is never uploaded anywhere.

What Setmark does not do

  • No network requests of any kind — Setmark works fully offline.
  • No accounts, sign-in, or user identifiers.
  • No analytics, crash reporting, or advertising SDKs.
  • No data is sold, shared, or transmitted to any third party.

Deleting your data

Uninstalling the app removes all locally stored data. Settings also includes a "Clear All History Data" option to erase your logged workouts without uninstalling.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes (for example, if a future version of Setmark adds an optional cloud-sync feature), this page will be updated and the "Last updated" date above will change accordingly.
